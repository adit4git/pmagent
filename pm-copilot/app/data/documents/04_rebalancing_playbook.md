# Rebalancing Playbook

**Document ID:** OPS-PLAY-002
**Owner:** Investments Operations

## Standard Rebalancing Workflow

1. **Identify drift.** Compare current allocation against target (see IPS section 1 for thresholds by risk profile).

2. **Check for tax-loss harvesting opportunities.** For taxable accounts, prioritize selling lots with losses or short-term gains expiring soon. Defer realizing long-term gains where possible.

3. **Apply ESG/restricted list filter.** Confirm that any new purchases are not on the restricted list. For ESG-mandated accounts, exclude flagged securities.

4. **Check sector concentration.** Ensure proposed trades do not push any sector above the limits in IPS section 2.

5. **Estimate transaction costs.** For trades over $50,000, compare expected costs across execution venues. Default to the firm's primary broker for trades under that threshold.

6. **Generate proposal.** Document the rationale, expected post-trade allocation, tax impact, and any policy exceptions in the standard rebalancing template.

7. **PM review and sign-off.** All proposals require PM sign-off before submission to the trading desk.

8. **Client communication.** For drift-driven rebalances within tolerance, summary in next quarterly review is sufficient. For mandatory rebalances or large discretionary changes, contact the client before execution.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Notes

- The wash-sale rule prohibits repurchasing the same or substantially identical security within 30 days of a sale at a loss.
- For ETF substitution during harvesting, maintain a documented "TLH partner list" (e.g., VTI ↔ ITOT, VXUS ↔ IXUS).
- Coordinate with the tax team for clients with complex trust structures.

## Common Pitfalls

- Forgetting the cash floor — leaves portfolios unable to meet distributions.
- Triggering short-term capital gains in taxable accounts during rebalancing.
- Rebalancing across the wash-sale window after a recent harvest.
