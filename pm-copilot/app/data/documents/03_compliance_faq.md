# Compliance FAQ for Portfolio Managers

**Document ID:** COMP-FAQ-2025
**Owner:** Compliance & Legal

## Q: Can I share specific trade recommendations directly with clients via email?

**A:** No. All trade recommendations must be communicated through the firm's documented advice channels (the Advisor Portal or in-person/phone reviews logged in the CRM). Email recommendations create supervisory and recordkeeping issues under SEC Rule 204-2 and FINRA recordkeeping requirements.

## Q: What is the firm's policy on AI-generated client communications?

**A:** AI-assisted drafts are permitted as starting points but must be reviewed and approved by the responsible portfolio manager before being shared with any client. AI tools may not autonomously send communications to clients. All AI-generated drafts must be flagged in the audit log.

## Q: When is human approval required for portfolio actions?

**A:** Required for:

- Any trade that would breach a stated client constraint or risk profile
- Any rebalancing proposal exceeding 5% of portfolio value
- Any new asset class introduction
- Any leverage or derivatives use

Not required for:

- Reading and summarizing client data
- Generating research summaries
- Drafting (but not sending) client communications

## Q: What client data can be sent to third-party services or APIs?

**A:** Personally identifiable information (PII) — full names, account numbers, SSNs, addresses, dates of birth — must never be transmitted to third-party AI services or external APIs without explicit written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer. Internal client identifiers (e.g., C001) and aggregated/anonymized data are acceptable.

## Q: What disclaimers must accompany AI-assisted analysis shared with clients?

**A:** Any analysis with AI involvement must include the standard disclaimer: "Analysis prepared with assistance from internal AI tools and reviewed by your portfolio manager. Past performance does not guarantee future results. This is not a personalized investment recommendation."
