# Acme Wealth — Investment Policy Statement (Excerpt)

**Document ID:** IPS-2024-v3
**Effective:** January 2024
**Owner:** Investment Committee

## 1. Asset Allocation Drift Tolerance

Portfolios are reviewed for allocation drift relative to client target allocations on a monthly basis. The following thresholds apply:

- **Conservative profiles:** ±3 percentage points per asset class triggers review; ±5 pp triggers mandatory rebalancing within 10 business days.
- **Moderate profiles:** ±5 pp triggers review; ±8 pp triggers mandatory rebalancing within 15 business days.
- **Aggressive profiles:** ±7 pp triggers review; ±10 pp triggers mandatory rebalancing within 20 business days.

Rebalancing decisions must consider tax implications, transaction costs, and the client's tax-loss harvesting flag.

## 2. Sector Concentration Limits

No single GICS sector may exceed the following share of total equity exposure without explicit client sign-off:

- Conservative: 25%
- Moderate: 30%
- Aggressive: 40%

Tech sector (Information Technology + Communication Services combined) is treated as a single concentration bucket for limits purposes given correlation patterns observed since 2020.

## 3. Cash Floor

All portfolios must hold a minimum cash buffer:

- Conservative: 5% of AUM
- Moderate: 2%
- Aggressive: 1%

Cash floors may be breached for up to 5 business days during rebalancing windows.

## 4. ESG and Mission-Aligned Mandates

Where a client has requested an ESG screen, the portfolio must exclude securities flagged in the firm's restricted list (maintained by the Compliance team). The current restricted list excludes companies with revenue exposure to: thermal coal extraction (>5%), controversial weapons, and tobacco production.

Mission-aligned mandates (e.g., the Garcia Foundation) require additional positive-screen criteria documented in the client's Statement of Investment Beliefs.
