# Internal Research — Tech Sector Outlook

**Document ID:** RES-2025-Q1-007
**Author:** Equity Research Desk
**Published:** January 2025
**Confidence:** Medium-High

## Summary

The Equity Research Desk maintains a **neutral-to-slightly-cautious** stance on US large-cap technology heading into the next two quarters, after a multi-year period of strong outperformance. Our view is driven by three factors:

1. **Valuation compression risk.** Forward P/E for the top-7 mega-cap tech names sits roughly 1.4 standard deviations above their 10-year median. Historically, returns from this percentile cluster have been below average over the subsequent 12 months.

2. **AI capex digestion.** We expect a near-term moderation in capital expenditure growth among hyperscalers as 2023–2024 buildouts are absorbed. This creates a short-term headwind for AI-infrastructure suppliers but is not a thesis-breaker.

3. **Regulatory overhang.** Multiple antitrust actions remain unresolved in both US and EU jurisdictions. The base case is no break-up, but tail risks have widened.

## Implications for Client Portfolios

- For **aggressive risk profiles** with existing tech overweights, we recommend trimming toward neutral over the next 60 days when tax-efficient.
- For **moderate profiles**, no action required if within the 30% sector concentration limit.
- For **conservative profiles**, ensure tech exposure remains below the 25% concentration cap.

## What Would Change Our View

- A meaningful pullback (>15%) bringing valuations back to historical norms.
- Clear evidence that AI revenue is materializing into operating leverage at the application layer, not just at the infrastructure layer.
- Resolution of major antitrust cases without forced divestitures.
